About me

  • I am currently a fourth-year student studying physics and math at the University of Virginia. I will graduate in May 2018, with a B.S in physics. My research interests are in Condensed Matter Theory, specifically topological phases. I have spent the last year working with an undergraduate research group at UVA studying bi-layer graphene under professor Teo. Recently I joined a High Energy Experimental group and have been working on upgrading a detector for the CMS experiment at CERN. In my free time I enjoy studying group theory and topology. Outside of class I am also an exec board member of both the Math club and the Society of Physics Students. I am an avid triathlete and spend a considerable amount of time at the gym running, cycling, swimming, and competing in races several times a year. I have spent the last four years practicing the drum set and I enjoy making music.
